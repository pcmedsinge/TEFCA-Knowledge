A practitioner's deep-dive · April 2026

TEFCA + FHIR + Clinical AI — what's actually happening, and what's actually changing.

You're a healthcare IT practitioner or clinician. You know what interoperability failure looks like from the inside — the duplicate CT, the conflicting med list, the patient who can't be matched across organizations.

This is a deep-dive on what TEFCA is in 2026 (mostly C-CDA over IHE) and what it's becoming (Facilitated FHIR via UDAP). No marketing language. Every claim sourced.

01 · The Problem Wall

Five things every healthcare IT practitioner has seen

Each scenario below is grounded in a peer-reviewed or institutional source. The point isn't to argue these problems exist — you've seen them. The point is to size them.

01

The repeat ED scan

A 54-year-old presents to Hospital A with abdominal pain. She had an abdominal CT three weeks ago at unaffiliated Hospital B across town. Hospital A's ED has no view into Hospital B's PACS or report. She gets another CT. The first one is sitting in a record twelve miles away.

14.7%
of ED CT scans are repeats — same study, same body region, within 30 days at unaffiliated EDs (CA + FL, 2007–2010)
HIE participation cut that by 59% for CT, 44% for ultrasound, 67% for chest x-ray.

Lammers EJ, Adler-Milstein J, Kocher KE. Medical Care. 2014;52(3):227-34.

02

The discharge med list nobody trusts

A 72-year-old is discharged after a CHF admission on nine medications. Her PCP's chart shows seven different ones. Her daughter brings in a third list from the assisted-living facility. Three caregivers, three lists, no source of truth. Everyone makes their best guess.

54%
of admitted patients have at least one potentially harmful medication discrepancy during their stay
75% of those discrepancies happen at discharge. 72% are rooted in errors taking the medication history — not in the reconciliation step itself.

Pippins JR, Gandhi TK, Hamann C, et al. Journal of General Internal Medicine. 2008;23(9):1414-1422.

03

The patient who can't be matched

A patient with a common name moves states and presents to a new health system. Their prior records exist within the destination QHIN's reach, but the demographic match isn't confident enough to return them. The new provider redoes the workup from scratch.

50%
match rates between organizations can drop this low — even between organizations using the same EHR vendor
Within a single facility, duplicate-record rates average 18%. 1 in 5 hospital CIOs report patient harm from mismatches in the prior year.

Pew Charitable Trusts. Enhanced Patient Matching Is Critical to Achieving Full Promise of Digital Health Records. October 2018.

04

The chronically ill patient seeing four specialists

A diabetic patient sees endocrinology, cardiology, nephrology, and primary care. Labs are duplicated quarterly. The cardiologist's diuretic adjustment doesn't make it back to the endocrinologist's chart. A preventable ED visit happens. Each provider holds half the picture.

$4,542
higher annual healthcare spending per chronically ill patient with highly fragmented PCP-referral patterns vs least-fragmented (US commercial claims, n=506,376)
Highly fragmented care: 32.8% vs 25.9% departures from clinical best practice and 9.1% vs 7.1% preventable hospitalization rate.

Frandsen BR, Joynt KE, Rebitzer JB, Jha AK. American Journal of Managed Care. 2015;21(5):355-62.

05

The C-CDA that arrived without the latest update

A specialist receives a C-CDA summary for a referred patient. The document is structurally valid. It just doesn't include the problem-list additions from last month's hospitalization across town — those exist in the originating EHR but never made it into the exchanged document. The first 20 minutes of the visit are reconstruction work.

2026
— and TEFCA exchange is still primarily document-based (C-CDA over IHE XCA/XCPD), not native FHIR
Facilitated FHIR SOP v2.0 (draft, March 2026) is the migration path; UDAP support extends through 2027. Until critical mass arrives, dual-pipeline reality and lossy round-trips between document and structured data.

2026 ASTP Annual Meeting recap; Facilitated FHIR Implementation SOP v2.0 (draft).
